What is agile?
Agile is an iterative approach to project management and software development that helps teams deliver value to customers faster and with less hassle
Agile methodology appeared in 2001 when the Manifesto was published. Agile.
Agile has 12 Manifesto principles:
  • The highest priority for us is to meet the needs customer, thanks to the regular and early delivery of valuable software security.
  • Changing requirements is welcome, even late in development. Agile processes allow you to use change to provide the customer competitive advantage.
  • A working product should be released as often as possible, with a frequency from a couple of weeks to a couple of months.
  • Throughout the project, developers and business representatives should work together daily.
  • Motivated professionals should work on the project. To work has been done, create conditions, provide support and fully trust them.
  • Direct communication is the most practical and effective a way to share information both with the team itself and within the team.
  • A working product is the main indicator of progress.
  • Investors, developers and users should be able to maintain a constant rhythm indefinitely. Agile helps to establish such sustainable development process.
  • Constant attention to technical excellence and quality design increases project flexibility.
  • Simplicity - the art of minimizing unnecessary work - is essential.
  • The best requirements, architectural and technical solutions are born in self-organizing teams.
  • The team should systematically analyze possible ways efficiency improvements and adjust accordingly style of your work.

Methodology Scrum
The concepts of Scrum and Agile are often confused because Scrum is built around the idea of constant improvement, which is the main principle of Agile
Scrum is a way of working, Agile is a way of thinking
Scrum is inherently heuristic. It is based on constant learning and adaptation to changeable factors
Scrum team
The composition of a scrum team has three separate roles: product owner, scrum master, and team developers.
Scrum Product Owner
  • They create and manage the product backlog.
  • They work closely with the company's management and the team, communicating to each participant the importance of working tasks in the product backlog.
  • They give the team clear instructions so that its members know which opportunities to put next.
  • They decide when to ship a product, aiming to do so as often as possible..
Scrum master
Scrum Masters ensure that the principles of Scrum are applied to their teams.
Scrum development team
Scrum teams do all the hard work. They are specialists in the principles of balanced development. Each team member has their own competencies Scrum teams create a plan for each sprint
Artifacts of Scrum
  • Product backlog
  • Sprint Backlog
  • Increment (or sprint goal)
Meetings or Scrum events
  • Backlog organization.
  • Sprint planning:
  • Sprint.
  • Daily Scrum meeting, or stand-up.
  • Stand up
Most often, within the framework of stand-up, each team member is asked to answer the following three questions, related to the achievement of the sprint goal:
  • "What did I manage to do yesterday?"
  • "What am I planning to do today?"
  • "Is there anything that can get in my way?"
Overview of the results of the sprint. At the end of the sprint, the team gathers to watch a demo of the increment (or to study it) in an informal setting
Sprint retrospective. A retrospective is held for the team to record and discuss all the successes and failures of a sprint, project, participants and their relationships, tools, or even specific meetings.

What is Kanban?
Kanban is a popular approach to implementing agile and DevOps principles in software development.
Although the Kanban methodology originated over 50 years ago, it is incredibly popular among modern Agile and DevOps development teams.
Kanban boards
The work of kanban teams is built around a kanban board, which is used for visualization and optimization workflow.
On a standard Kanban board, the process consists of three steps: Planned, In Progress, and Done.
Kanban cards
Kanban literally means "visual signal" in Japanese. For teams using Kanban, each work task is presented as a separate card on the board.
Benefits of Kanban
Kanban is one of the most popular software development methodologies used by agile teams today
Planning flexibility
The Kanban team only focuses on the current work. Upon completion of the work task, the team picks up next task from the top of the backlog.
Less bottlenecks
Multitasking kills efficiency.
Reducing Cycle Time Cycle time is a key metric for Kanban teams.
visibility
One of the main values is the utmost attention to improving the efficiency of the team with each work iteration
Continuous supply
Continuous delivery (CD) involves the frequent delivery of product releases to customers.
